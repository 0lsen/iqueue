About

This is a simple (read: fugly) simulation of a Queueing System:
  • There is one queue, comprised of food items, that are to be eaten by a couple of consumers.
  • At each step a random amount of food items falls into the queue and each consumer (in random order) will attempt to eat a random number of items.
  • Consumers and food are split into three categories: vegan (green), vegetarian (yellow) and omnivorous (red) - indicating what kinds of food they are able to eat.
    E.g. omnivorous consumers are able to eat all kinds of food, vegetarians cannot consume "red" food and vegans are limited to "green" food.
  • each food item comes with a couple of properties:
    • a step at which it will spoil
    • a number of calories it provides
    • a category denoting who can consume it
  • The challenge is to create an algorithm (in Food.ts:score()) that will sort the queue in a manner that will satify your personal needs.
    You will most likely want to avoid spoilage and adhere to some order of hard and soft constraints.
    For example:
    • hard constraint: avoid spoilage and adhere to some scheduling policy (like SJN or HRRN)
    • soft constraint: prefer food with more calories
    • other useful stuff, like assigning higher priority to meat as it can be eaten by fewer consumers
  • Default: sort by HRRN, that's it.
Notes:
  • 1 Normal Distribution assumed
  • 2 Poisson Distribution assumed
